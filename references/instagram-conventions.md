# Instagram Caption Conventions

Guidelines for creating Instagram captions that drive engagement and complement visual content.

---

## Instagram Caption Format

### Format defaults
- **Length:** 125-150 characters for maximum engagement (appears fully in feed without "more" button), OR 1,000-2,200 characters for storytelling captions
- **Visual-first platform:** The image/video is primary. Caption supports and adds context.
- **Line breaks matter:** Use line breaks every 2-3 lines for mobile readability
- **Emoji usage:** 3-5 emojis is standard on Instagram. Use them for visual breaks and emphasis.
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 highly relevant hashtags. Place in first comment or at the end after line breaks.

### Structure patterns

**Pattern 1: Short and punchy (125-150 chars)**
```
Most founders waste 6 months building features nobody wants.

We validate ideas in 3 weeks instead. 

Link in bio for the full framework. 👆
```

**Pattern 2: Story format (1,000-2,200 chars)**
```
This founder spent $80K building a platform nobody wanted. 😬

8 months of development. Beautiful code. Zero users.

Here's what went wrong:

They built for 6 months without talking to a single customer. They assumed they knew the problem. They were wrong.

Here's what we do differently:

→ 20 customer interviews before writing code
→ Landing page + waitlist validation  
→ Paid pilot with 3-5 customers

The result? We validate ideas in 3 weeks instead of 6 months.

Real example: A healthcare startup came to us with a similar situation. We interviewed 30 providers in 2 weeks, found a different problem they'd actually pay to solve, and built a focused MVP in 6 weeks.

6 months later: $120K in revenue, 40 customers, and a clear path to scale. 📈

The lesson: Talk to customers before you build, not after.

What's the biggest mistake you've made in product development? Drop it in the comments. 👇

---

#StartupDevelopment #MVPDevelopment #ProductValidation #StartupAdvice #TechStartups
```

**Pattern 3: List format with emojis**
```
3 questions we ask before writing any code: 🤔

1️⃣ Who will pay for this?
2️⃣ What's the smallest version they'd pay for?
3️⃣ Can we build it in 6 weeks?

This framework has saved our clients over $2M in unnecessary development.

Swipe for the full breakdown →

---

#StartupTips #ProductDevelopment #MVPStrategy
```

**Pattern 4: Behind-the-scenes**
```
Behind the scenes of a discovery sprint 👀

We just wrapped a session with a fintech startup. The founder wanted to build 15 features. We convinced him to start with 3.

Here's the framework:
→ List every feature
→ Ask: "Would customers pay for this product with ONLY this feature?"
→ Build the ones that got a "yes"

Result: 6-week timeline instead of 6 months. 🚀

Sometimes the best development work is the code you don't write.

What feature have you cut from your product? Tell us below. 👇
```

### Voice guidelines
- **Conversational and casual:** Instagram is more personal than LinkedIn
- **Use emojis naturally:** They're expected on Instagram but should feel authentic
- **First-person storytelling:** "We" and "I" create connection
- **Ask questions:** Captions that invite comments get more reach
- **Be authentic:** Instagram rewards genuine, personal content over polished corporate messaging

### Caption structure for longer posts
1. **Hook (first line):** Appears in feed preview. Make it count.
2. **Story or context (2-4 paragraphs):** Tell the story with specific details
3. **Key insights (bulleted with →):** Make it scannable
4. **Proof point or example:** Specific numbers or case study
5. **Call to action:** Question for comments or link to bio
6. **Line breaks + hashtags:** Separate hashtags from caption body

## Instagram-specific tactics

### Hashtag strategy
- **Use 3-5 targeted hashtags** (not 30 - that's outdated advice)
- **Mix sizes:** 1 large (100K+ posts), 2-3 medium (10K-100K posts), 1-2 niche (1K-10K posts)
- **Place in first comment or after line breaks** in caption
- **Rotate hashtags:** Don't use the same set every time
- **Create a branded hashtag:** For your company/campaign

**Example hashtag set for a startup development post:**
```
#StartupDevelopment (large - 150K posts)
#MVPDevelopment (medium - 25K posts)
#ProductValidation (medium - 18K posts)
#StartupAdvice (medium - 45K posts)
#TechStartups (niche - 8K posts)
```

### Engagement tactics
- **Respond to every comment in the first hour:** Instagram's algorithm rewards quick engagement
- **Ask a question in the caption:** Posts with questions get 50% more comments
- **Use Instagram Stories to drive traffic:** Share the post to your story with a "New post 👆" sticker
- **Tag relevant accounts:** When mentioning someone's work, tag them in the caption or photo
- **Post when your audience is active:** Check Instagram Insights for your best times

### Link strategy
- **"Link in bio"** is the standard CTA since Instagram doesn't allow clickable links in captions
- **Use link-in-bio tools:** Linktree, Later, or custom landing pages
- **Update bio link regularly:** Match it to your most recent post
- **Stories with 10K+ followers:** Use the "swipe up" or link sticker feature

## Instagram content types

### Educational carousel
Multi-image posts that teach a framework or process. Each slide builds on the previous one.

**Caption example:**
```
The 3-week validation sprint we use with every client 📊

This approach has saved our clients an average of 4 months of speculative development.

What's your validation process? Drop it in the comments. 👇

---

Share a specific client story with before/after or problem/solution structure.

**Caption example:**
```

A healthcare startup came to us after building for 8 months with zero users.

Here's what we did:
→ Interviewed 30 healthcare providers in 2 weeks
→ Found a different problem they'd actually pay to solve

### Behind-the-scenes
Show your process, team, or how you work. People connect with authenticity.

**Caption example:**
```
What a discovery sprint looks like 👀

3 days. 1 whiteboard. 47 sticky notes. And a completely different product direction.

This is how we help founders avoid building the wrong thing.

We don't start with code. We start with conversations.

What's your product development process? Share below. 👇
```

### Thought leadership
Share an opinion or contrarian take. Be specific and back it with experience.

**Caption example:**
```

A responsive web app works fine until you have 50K+ users and a clear reason for native features.

It's "What problem does a native app solve that web doesn't?"

Agree or disagree? Let's discuss. 👇

---

#StartupAdvice #MobileApp #ProductStrategy
```

## Visual content guidelines

### Image specifications
- **Aspect ratio:** 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait) for feed posts
- **Resolution:** Minimum 1080 x 1080 pixels (square) or 1080 x 1350 (portrait)
- **File size:** Under 30MB for images
- **Format:** JPG or PNG

### Carousel posts (multi-image)
- **Up to 10 images/videos** per post
- **Use for tutorials, step-by-step guides, or storytelling**
- **First image is crucial:** It appears in feed and determines if people swipe
- **Consistent design:** Keep visual style cohesive across slides

### Video posts
- **Length:** 3-60 seconds for feed posts, up to 60 minutes for IGTV
- **Aspect ratio:** 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait)
- **Captions/subtitles:** 85% of videos watched without sound
- **Hook in first 3 seconds:** Most people decide to keep watching immediately

### Reels
- **Length:** 15-90 seconds
- **Vertical format:** 9:16 aspect ratio
- **Trending audio:** Use popular sounds for better reach
- **Text overlays:** Add captions and key points as text on video
- **Hook immediately:** First 1-2 seconds determine if people keep watching

## What to avoid

### AI tells on Instagram
- Overly formal language - Instagram is casual
- Em dashes (—) - Use periods or line breaks instead
- "Delve," "landscape," "crucial," "robust," "seamless," "leverage"
- Perfect grammar - Fragments and casual language feel more authentic
- No emojis - Instagram culture expects some emoji use

### Engagement-bait patterns Instagram penalizes
- "Tag someone who needs to see this"
- "Double tap if you agree"
- "Comment 'YES' for the link"
- "Follow for more" in every caption
- Asking for likes/follows explicitly

### Other mistakes
- **30 hashtags** - Outdated advice that looks spammy now
- **Generic stock photos** - Use authentic, real images
- **No line breaks** - Makes captions hard to read on mobile
- **Ignoring comments** - Algorithm rewards engagement
- **Posting at random times** - Check your Insights for best times

## Instagram vs. other platforms

**Instagram vs. Facebook:**
- More visual-focused (image/video is primary)
- Shorter captions generally perform better
- Younger demographic (18-34 vs. Facebook's 25-54)
- Hashtags are more important on Instagram
- Stories are more active on Instagram

**Instagram vs. Twitter:**
- Much longer captions allowed (2,200 chars vs. 280)
- Visual content is required
- More personal/lifestyle content
- Hashtags work differently (3-5 targeted vs. 1-2 on Twitter)

**Instagram vs. LinkedIn:**
- More casual, less professional
- Personal stories and behind-the-scenes content
- Emojis are expected
- Visual content is required
- Younger audience

## Shortcode handling

If the user has defined shortcodes in their profile:
- `{{booking_link}}` → "Link in bio" (can't use direct links in captions)
- `{{case_study:name}}` → "Link in bio for full case study"
- `{{cta:soft}}` → Adapt to Instagram's casual, question-based CTAs

Instagram CTAs should be question-based or bio-directed: "What's your experience? 👇" or "Full guide in bio 👆"

## Research and inspiration mode

When asked to create Instagram captions from a URL or article:

1. **Visual first:** What image/video would accompany this caption?
2. **Extract the hook:** What's the most interesting/surprising point for the first line?
3. **Make it personal:** Instagram rewards authentic, personal storytelling
4. **Add a question:** Invite comments and discussion
5. **Keep it scannable:** Use line breaks, emojis, and → bullets
6. **Plan hashtags:** 3-5 relevant, targeted hashtags
7. **Check for AI tells:** Run the anti-AI checklist

Don't just summarize the article. Find the Instagram-worthy moment - the visual story, the personal insight, the behind-the-scenes detail.
