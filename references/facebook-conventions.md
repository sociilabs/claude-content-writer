# Facebook Content Conventions

Guidelines for creating Facebook posts that drive engagement across business pages and personal profiles.

---

## Facebook Post Format

### Format defaults
- **Length:** 100-250 characters for maximum reach. Facebook's algorithm favors shorter posts that generate comments and shares.
- **Longer posts (250-500 characters):** Use when telling a story or sharing a case study. Include line breaks for readability.
- **Visual-first:** Facebook is highly visual. Posts with images/videos get 2.3x more engagement than text-only.
- **Link placement:** Put links in the first comment, not the post body. Facebook suppresses reach on posts with external links.
- **Emoji usage:** 1-3 relevant emojis can increase engagement, but don't overdo it. Use strategically for emphasis or visual breaks.

### Structure patterns

**Pattern 1: Question hook**
```
What's the biggest mistake you see founders make with their first hire?

For us, it's hiring for "potential" instead of "can do the job today."

Early-stage startups don't have time to train. You need someone who's done it before.

[Share your experience in the comments 👇]
```

**Pattern 2: Story format**
```
A founder reached out last week after spending $80K on a platform nobody wanted.

The problem? They built for 6 months without talking to a single customer.

Here's what we do differently:
→ 20 customer interviews before writing code
→ Landing page + waitlist validation
→ Paid pilot with 3-5 customers

Saves months of wasted development.

[Link to full case study in comments]
```

**Pattern 3: Insight + call to action**
```
Your MVP doesn't need real-time features. 🎯

Async + polling works fine until 10K users.

Don't optimize for scale you don't have yet. Build for the customers in front of you, not the ones you hope to have.

What features have you over-engineered? Drop a comment.
```

**Pattern 4: List format**
```
3 questions we ask before writing any code:

1️⃣ Who will pay for this?
2️⃣ What's the smallest version they'd pay for?
3️⃣ Can we build it in 6 weeks?

This framework has saved our clients over $2M in unnecessary development.

Want the full breakdown? Link in comments.
```

### Voice guidelines
- **Conversational:** Write like you're talking to a friend, not presenting at a conference.
- **Personal stories work:** Facebook rewards authentic, personal content over corporate messaging.
- **Ask questions:** Posts that generate comments get more reach. Ask genuine questions that invite discussion.
- **Use "you" and "I":** First and second person create connection.
- **Avoid corporate jargon:** "We help founders" > "We provide comprehensive solutions for startup founders."

### Engagement tactics
- **Respond to every comment in the first hour:** Facebook's algorithm rewards quick engagement.
- **Ask follow-up questions in replies:** Keep conversations going.
- **Tag relevant people:** When mentioning someone's work or idea, tag them (but don't spam tags for visibility).
- **Use Facebook's native features:** Polls, questions, and photo carousels get algorithmic boosts.
- **Post when your audience is active:** Check Facebook Insights for your specific audience's peak times.

## Facebook Post Types

### Educational post
Share a framework, process, or lesson learned. Include specific steps or numbered points.

**Example:**
```
The 3-week validation sprint we use with every client:

Week 1: Customer interviews
→ 20 conversations minimum
→ Focus on their current process and pain points
→ Goal: Find the expensive problem

Week 2: Landing page + waitlist
→ Build a simple page describing the solution
→ Drive 100-200 visitors
→ Target: 20%+ email capture rate

Week 3: Paid pilot
→ Offer to build a basic version for 3-5 customers
→ Charge something (even $500)
→ If nobody will pay, you don't have a business yet

This approach has saved our clients an average of 4 months of speculative development.

Full breakdown in the comments 👇
```

### Case study post
Tell a specific client story with numbers and outcomes. Use the story structure: problem → approach → result.

**Example:**
```
A healthcare startup came to us after 8 months of development with zero users.

They'd built a beautiful platform. The problem? Nobody wanted it.

Here's what we did:
→ Interviewed 30 healthcare providers in 2 weeks
→ Found a different problem they'd actually pay to solve
→ Built a focused MVP in 6 weeks
→ Launched to 5 paid pilot customers

6 months later: $120K in revenue, 40 customers, and a clear path to scale.

The lesson: Talk to customers before you build, not after.

[Full case study linked in comments]
```

### Behind-the-scenes post
Share your process, team culture, or how you work. People connect with authenticity.

**Example:**
```
We just wrapped a discovery sprint with a fintech startup.

The founder wanted to build 15 features. We convinced him to start with 3.

Here's the framework we used:
1. List every feature
2. Ask: "Would customers pay for this product with ONLY this feature?"
3. Build the ones that got a "yes"

Result: 6-week timeline instead of 6 months. Launch in 3 weeks.

Sometimes the best development work is the code you don't write.
```

### Thought leadership post
Share an opinion or contrarian take on your industry. Be specific and back it with experience.

**Example:**
```
Unpopular opinion: Most startups don't need a mobile app.

A responsive web app works fine until you have 50K+ users and a clear reason for native features.

We've saved clients $100K+ by starting with web and only building native when the data justified it.

The question isn't "Should we build an app?" It's "What problem does a native app solve that web doesn't?"

Agree or disagree? Let's discuss 👇
```

## Publishing guidelines

### Timing
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times:** 9 AM - 11 AM, 1 PM - 3 PM (in your audience's timezone)
- **Avoid:** Late evenings, weekends (unless your audience is B2C)

### First-hour engagement strategy
The first 60 minutes determine your post's reach:

1. **Post the link in the first comment immediately**
2. **Respond to every comment within 60 minutes**
3. **Ask follow-up questions in replies** to keep conversations going
4. **Have 2-3 colleagues/team members comment early** (genuine comments, not "Great post!")
5. **Share to relevant Facebook groups** (if appropriate and allowed)

### Hashtags
- **Use 1-3 hashtags maximum** on Facebook (unlike Instagram)
- Place them at the end of the post or in the first comment
- Mix broad (#Startups) with specific (#MVPDevelopment)
- Don't use hashtags in every post - Facebook's algorithm doesn't weight them heavily

## What to avoid

### AI tells on Facebook
- Em dashes (—) - Use periods or line breaks instead
- "Delve," "landscape," "crucial," "robust," "seamless," "leverage"
- Overly formal language - Facebook is conversational
- Perfect grammar - Fragments and casual language feel more authentic
- Emoji overload - 1-3 is good, 10+ looks spammy

### Engagement-bait patterns Facebook penalizes
- "Tag someone who needs to see this"
- "Share if you agree"
- "Comment 'YES' if you want the link"
- "I'll send you the guide if you comment"
- Asking for reactions/shares explicitly

### Other mistakes
- **External links in post body** - Kills reach by 40-50%
- **Too many hashtags** - Looks spammy
- **Posting the same content to multiple groups** - Facebook detects and suppresses this
- **Text-only posts consistently** - Mix in images, videos, and visual content
- **Corporate speak** - "Solutions," "leverage," "synergy" don't work on Facebook

## Visual content guidelines

### Images
- **Minimum size:** 1200 x 630 pixels
- **Text on image:** Keep under 20% of image area (Facebook's old rule still affects reach)
- **Use real photos over stock** - Authentic images get more engagement
- **Include faces when possible** - Posts with faces get 38% more likes

### Videos
- **Length:** 1-3 minutes for maximum completion rate
- **Square format (1:1)** works best for mobile
- **Add captions** - 85% of Facebook videos are watched without sound
- **Hook in first 3 seconds** - Most people decide to keep watching in the first 3 seconds

## Shortcode handling

If the user has defined shortcodes in their profile:
- `{{booking_link}}` → Replace with actual URL (in first comment, not post body)
- `{{case_study:name}}` → Replace with case study URL (in first comment)
- `{{cta:soft}}` → Adapt to Facebook's conversational tone

Facebook CTAs should be softer and more discussion-oriented: "What's your experience with this?" > "Book a call at [link]."

## Research and inspiration mode

When asked to create Facebook posts from a URL or article:

1. **Extract the story or insight:** What's the most interesting/surprising/useful point?
2. **Make it conversational:** Facebook is more casual than LinkedIn or blog content
3. **Add a question or discussion prompt:** Facebook rewards posts that generate comments
4. **Keep it concise:** 100-250 characters for maximum reach
5. **Plan for visual:** Suggest an image or video to accompany the post
6. **Check for AI tells:** Run the anti-AI checklist even on short-form content

Don't just summarize the article. Find the human story or insight and make it conversational.

## Facebook vs. other platforms

**Facebook vs. LinkedIn:**
- More casual, less corporate
- Personal stories > professional achievements
- Questions and discussions > thought leadership
- Emojis are more acceptable

**Facebook vs. Twitter:**
- Longer form (100-250 chars vs. Twitter's 240-280)
- More storytelling, less punchy one-liners
- Visual content is more important
- Algorithm favors engagement (comments) over virality (shares)

**Facebook vs. Instagram:**
- More text-focused (Instagram is visual-first)
- Longer captions work on Facebook
- Links in comments work on Facebook (Instagram limits this)
- Older demographic on Facebook (25-54 vs. Instagram's 18-34)
