# Content Writing Frameworks & Best Practices

This document contains proven content writing frameworks and methodologies used by professional copywriters and content strategists. These frameworks are integrated into the Content Writer skill to ensure high-quality, persuasive, and effective content generation.

---

## Core Copywriting Frameworks

### 1. AIDA Framework
**Best for:** Sales pages, landing pages, email campaigns, ads

**Structure:**
- **Attention** - Hook the reader immediately with a compelling headline or opening
- **Interest** - Build interest by highlighting relevant benefits or pain points
- **Desire** - Create desire by showing transformation and social proof
- **Action** - Clear call-to-action that tells them exactly what to do next

**When to use:** When you need to move readers from awareness to action quickly. Perfect for conversion-focused content.

---

### 2. PAS Framework (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
**Best for:** Blog posts, email sequences, sales copy

**Structure:**
- **Problem** - Identify the reader's pain point clearly
- **Agitate** - Make the problem feel urgent and costly to ignore
- **Solution** - Present your offer as the answer they've been looking for

**When to use:** When addressing a specific pain point. Particularly effective for problem-aware audiences.

---

### 3. BAB Framework (Before-After-Bridge)
**Best for:** Case studies, testimonials, transformation stories

**Structure:**
- **Before** - Paint a picture of life with the problem
- **After** - Show the transformation and better future state
- **Bridge** - Explain how your solution creates that transformation

**When to use:** When you want to emphasize transformation and results. Great for case studies and success stories.

---

### 4. FAB Framework (Features-Advantages-Benefits)
**Best for:** Product descriptions, service pages, technical content

**Structure:**
- **Features** - What it is (the facts)
- **Advantages** - What it does (how it works)
- **Benefits** - What it means for them (the outcome)

**When to use:** When explaining products or services, especially technical ones. Bridges the gap between features and emotional benefits.

---

### 5. The 4 C's Framework
**Best for:** Clear, concise communication across all formats

**Structure:**
- **Clear** - Easy to understand, no jargon
- **Concise** - No fluff, every word earns its place
- **Compelling** - Engaging and persuasive
- **Credible** - Backed by proof, authority, and trust signals

**When to use:** Universal framework for any content type. Use as a quality checklist.

## Long-Form Content Frameworks

### 6. LEMA Framework
**Best for:** Authority articles, thought leadership, educational content

**Structure:**
- **Logic** - Ideas flow naturally, answers follow questions
- **Explicitness** - Concrete and specific, no vague language
- **Memorability** - Use analogies, stories, coined terms that stick
- **Actionability** - Clear next steps, pathway to results

**When to use:** When building authority and creating content that readers remember and share. Excellent for TOFU (top-of-funnel) content.

---

### 7. The 4-Point Content Framework
**Best for:** Blog posts, articles, long-form content

**Structure:**
1. **Hook Attention Early** - Compelling headline and intro
2. **Make It Effortless to Read** - Formatting, subheadings, whitespace
3. **Maintain Narrative Flow** - Smooth transitions, logical progression
4. **Polish Ruthlessly** - Cut fluff, strengthen CTA, add finishing touches

**When to use:** For any long-form content where engagement and dwell time matter. Optimizes for how people actually read online.

---

### 8. CONVERT Framework
**Best for:** Conversion-focused content, lead generation

**Structure:**
- **C - Clarity** - Simple language, easy to understand
- **O - Outcome** - Show the end result clearly
- **N - Narrative** - Tell a story, create connection
- **V - Value** - Demonstrate clear value proposition
- **E - Engagement** - Interactive elements, questions, hooks
- **R - Relevance** - Speaks directly to target audience
- **T - Trigger** - Strong CTA that prompts action

**When to use:** When conversion is the primary goal. Ensures every element drives toward action.

## Social Media Best Practices

### Brand Voice Consistency
- Maintain consistent tone across all platforms
- Adapt voice to context (customer service vs. promotional)
- Document voice guidelines (adjectives, phrases, things to avoid)

### Platform-Specific Writing

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional but personable
- Lead with value or insight
- Use line breaks for readability
- Link in first comment
- 1,400-1,800 characters optimal

**Twitter/X:**
- Concise and punchy
- One clear idea per tweet
- Use threads for longer narratives
- 240-280 characters for single tweets
- No external links in tweet body

**Instagram:**
- Visual-first, caption-second
- First 125 characters are critical (above fold)
- Use line breaks and emojis strategically
- 3-5 relevant hashtags
- "Link in bio" for CTAs

**Facebook:**
- Conversational and community-focused
- 100-250 characters for maximum reach
- Link in first comment for better algorithm performance
- Ask questions to drive engagement

## Content Quality Checklist

### Before Publishing, Verify:

**Clarity**
- [ ] Can a 10-year-old understand the main point?
- [ ] Is every sentence necessary?
- [ ] Are technical terms explained?

**Structure**
- [ ] Does the intro hook immediately?
- [ ] Do ideas flow logically?
- [ ] Are transitions smooth?
- [ ] Is the CTA clear and compelling?

**Engagement**
- [ ] Are there subheadings every 200-300 words?
- [ ] Are paragraphs 2-3 lines maximum?
- [ ] Are there bullet points or lists?
- [ ] Is there whitespace for visual relief?

**Persuasion**
- [ ] Is there social proof or credibility?
- [ ] Are benefits emphasized over features?
- [ ] Is there a clear value proposition?
- [ ] Does it address objections?

**Voice**
- [ ] Does it sound human, not AI?
- [ ] Is the brand voice consistent?
- [ ] Are there specific examples and details?
- [ ] Does it avoid AI tells (leverage, seamless, robust, etc.)?

## Content Generation Process

### Phase 1: Discuss (Requirements Gathering)
**Goal:** Understand what to write and why

**Questions to answer:**
- What's the topic and angle?
- Who's the target audience?
- What's the primary goal (awareness, consideration, conversion)?
- What framework fits best?
- What tone and style?
- What length and format?
- What CTAs or next steps?

### Phase 2: Plan (Research & Strategy)
**Goal:** Create a detailed execution plan

**Activities:**
- Research topic and gather insights
- Identify key points and structure
- Select appropriate framework
- Outline sections and flow
- Plan examples, data, proof points
- Define SEO strategy (if applicable)

### Phase 3: Execute (Content Generation)
**Goal:** Write the content following the plan

**Process:**
- Apply selected framework
- Write with clarity and specificity
- Use active voice and concrete examples
- Maintain consistent brand voice
- Follow platform conventions
- Integrate SEO naturally

### Phase 4: Verify (Quality Check)
**Goal:** Ensure quality and effectiveness

**Checks:**
- Run anti-AI audit (humanizer)
- Check SEO optimization (claude-seo)
- Verify framework adherence
- Check clarity and flow
- Validate CTAs and next steps
- Proofread for errors

### Phase 5: Ship (Finalize & Deliver)
**Goal:** Save and present the content

**Actions:**
- Save to appropriate directory
- Generate metadata file
- Present to user with publishing notes
- Provide platform-specific recommendations

## Framework Selection Guide

**Choose AIDA when:**
- Writing sales or landing pages
- Creating email campaigns
- Need quick conversion

**Choose PAS when:**
- Addressing specific pain points
- Writing problem-aware content
- Need urgency and emotion

**Choose BAB when:**
- Showcasing transformations
- Writing case studies
- Emphasizing before/after

**Choose LEMA when:**
- Building thought leadership
- Creating educational content
- Want memorable, shareable content

**Choose 4-Point when:**
- Writing long-form articles
- Optimizing for engagement
- Need strong narrative flow

**Choose CONVERT when:**
- Conversion is primary goal
- Lead generation focus
- Need comprehensive persuasion

## Anti-AI Writing Principles

### Avoid These AI Tells:
- Overused words: leverage, seamless, robust, pivotal, delve, realm
- Rule of three everywhere
- Parallel list structures
- Em dash overuse (—)
- Throat-clearing openers ("In today's digital landscape...")
- Generic conclusions ("In conclusion, it's clear that...")
- Vague attributions ("Studies show...")
- Superficial -ing analyses

### Instead, Write Like a Human:
- Use specific examples and numbers
- Vary sentence structure naturally
- Include personal observations
- Use contractions and casual language (when appropriate)
- Add unexpected details or asides
- Show personality and quirks
- Reference specific sources
- Use concrete imagery

## SEO Integration

### On-Page SEO Checklist:
- [ ] Target keyword in title (naturally)
- [ ] Target keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Keyword variations throughout (natural density)
- [ ] Descriptive subheadings (H2, H3)
- [ ] Internal links to related content
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources
- [ ] Meta title (50-60 characters)
- [ ] Meta description (150-160 characters)
- [ ] Alt text for images
- [ ] URL slug optimization

### Content SEO Best Practices:
- Write for humans first, search engines second
- Answer the search intent completely
- Use natural language and variations
- Include related topics and entities
- Structure with clear hierarchy
- Make content scannable
- Aim for comprehensive coverage
- Update regularly

## Platform-Specific Optimization

### Blog Articles
- **Length:** 1,500-2,500 words for SEO
- **Structure:** H2 every 300-400 words
- **Paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences maximum
- **Images:** Every 300-500 words
- **CTAs:** Mid-content and end
- **Links:** 3-5 internal, 1-2 external

### LinkedIn Posts
- **Length:** 1,400-1,800 characters
- **Structure:** Hook → Value → CTA
- **Formatting:** Line breaks every 2-3 lines
- **Links:** First comment only
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant ones
- **Engagement:** Ask questions

### Twitter Threads
- **Length:** 5-12 tweets optimal
- **Structure:** Hook → Build → Payoff → CTA
- **Formatting:** One idea per tweet
- **Links:** Final tweet only
- **Engagement:** Numbered tweets (1/10)
- **Timing:** Post all at once

### Instagram Captions
- **Length:** 125 chars (short) or 1,000-2,200 (story)
- **Structure:** Hook → Story → CTA
- **Formatting:** Line breaks, emojis
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 in caption or first comment
- **CTA:** "Link in bio"
- **Engagement:** Questions, polls

## Voice Calibration

### Analyzing Voice from URLs:
When scanning URLs for tone detection, analyze:

**Sentence Structure:**
- Average sentence length
- Simple vs. complex sentences
- Sentence variety and rhythm

**Vocabulary:**
- Technical vs. casual language
- Industry jargon usage
- Unique phrases or expressions

**Tone Markers:**
- Formal vs. informal
- Serious vs. playful
- Direct vs. diplomatic
- Confident vs. cautious

**Personality Indicators:**
- Use of humor or wit
- Personal anecdotes
- Opinions and perspectives
- Emotional language

**Formatting Preferences:**
- Paragraph length
- Use of lists and bullets
- Subheading style
- Emphasis techniques (bold, italics)
